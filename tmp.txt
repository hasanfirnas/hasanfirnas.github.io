	•	Definition:
	•	The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) is a globally recognized set of security standards established by the PCI Security Standards Council (PCI SSC). It’s designed to ensure secure processing, storing, and transmission of cardholder data.
	•	Why it Matters:
	•	PCI DSS aims to protect sensitive cardholder data and reduce the risk of security breaches.
	•	Compliance is mandatory for all entities involved in payment card processing, including merchants, service providers, and payment processors.
	•	PCI DSS 4.0 Core Objectives:
	1.	Secure Network & Systems: Protect networks and systems to maintain a secure environment.
	2.	Protect Cardholder Data: Ensure that cardholder data is protected at rest and in transit.
	3.	Maintain Vulnerability Management: Continuously identify and address security vulnerabilities.
	4.	Implement Strong Access Controls: Restrict access to cardholder data on a need-to-know basis.
	5.	Regular Monitoring & Testing: Monitor networks and test security systems for effectiveness.
	6.	Maintain an Information Security Policy: Establish and enforce security policies to guide the protection of data.
